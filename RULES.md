# Workspace Rules

These rules help keep our cooperative workspace safe, functional, and welcoming for all members.

## General Conduct

### Respect and Inclusivity
- Treat all members, guests, and staff with respect
- This is an inclusive space - discrimination, harassment, or hate speech will not be tolerated
- Keep noise at reasonable levels
- Share space and tools fairly
- Clean up your work area when finished

### Membership
- Keep your membership current and dues paid
- Sign in on the member log each visit
- Guests must be accompanied by a member at all times
- Members are responsible for their guests' behavior
- Follow the posted hours - no unauthorized after-hours access

## Safety Rules

### Personal Safety
- **Safety glasses are required in the workshop area at all times**
- Wear appropriate clothing:
  - No loose clothing or jewelry near power tools
  - Closed-toe shoes required
  - Tie back long hair
  - Hearing protection recommended for power tools
- No working while impaired (alcohol, drugs, or excessive fatigue)
- Know where the first aid kit and fire extinguisher are located
- Report all injuries immediately, no matter how minor

### Tool Safety
- **Only use tools you've been trained on**
- Power tools require safety certification - no exceptions
- Never disable or bypass safety guards
- Unplug power tools before changing blades or bits
- Use the right tool for the job
- Inspect tools before use - report damaged tools immediately
- Keep work area clean and well-lit

### Workshop Safety
- No running in the workspace
- Keep aisles and exits clear
- No eating or drinking in the workshop area (break room only)
- Clean up spills immediately
- Proper disposal of waste materials
- No blocking fire exits or safety equipment

## Tool Usage

### General Guidelines
- Return tools to their designated locations
- Clean tools after use
- Sign out tools on the checkout sheet if borrowing
- Report broken or dull tools - don't just put them back
- Don't modify workshop tools without approval
- Last person to use a tool is responsible for its condition

### Power Tools
- Complete required safety training before use
- One person per power tool at a time
- Don't distract someone using a power tool
- Wait for blades/bits to stop completely before walking away
- Unplug tools when not in use
- Report any unusual sounds, vibrations, or behavior

### Personal Tools
- Label your personal tools clearly
- Don't leave personal tools in communal areas
- Workshop not responsible for lost/stolen personal items
- Rent a locker if you need secure storage

## Materials and Storage

### Communal Materials
- Small amounts of scraps, fasteners, and consumables are free
- Log larger material usage on the materials sheet
- Replace or pay for materials if requested
- Don't hoard materials - take only what you need

### Project Storage
- **Short-term (under 2 weeks)**: Use labeled shelf space
- **Long-term**: Rent a project locker ($10/month)
- **Label everything** with your name and date
- Unlabeled items may be disposed of after 2 weeks
- Never store projects in aisles or work surfaces
- Hazardous materials require prior approval

### Waste Disposal
- Separate trash, recycling, and wood scraps
- Dispose of hazardous materials properly (see shop manager)
- Empty dust collectors after heavy use
- Take large project waste home or to proper disposal

## Facilities

### Cleanliness
- Clean your work area after each session
- Sweep up sawdust and debris
- Wipe down surfaces
- Put tools back where they belong
- Participate in monthly cleanup days

### Shared Spaces
- Break room: clean up after yourself, label food in fridge
- Bathroom: keep it clean for the next person
- Common areas: no monopolizing spaces for long periods
- Computer station: 30-minute limit when others are waiting

## Violations and Enforcement

### Warning System
1. **First offense**: Verbal warning and education
2. **Second offense**: Written warning and possible temporary suspension
3. **Third offense**: Membership review by board

### Immediate Suspension
The following may result in immediate suspension pending review:
- Safety violations that endanger others
- Theft or vandalism
- Violence or threats
- Harassment or discrimination
- Using tools while impaired
- Allowing unauthorized persons to use tools

### Appeals
Members may appeal disciplinary actions to the board within 14 days.

## Emergency Procedures

### Fire
1. Activate fire alarm
2. Evacuate immediately via nearest exit
3. Call 911
4. Assemble at designated meeting point
5. Do not re-enter until cleared by fire department

### Medical Emergency
1. Call 911 for serious injuries
2. Notify shop manager or senior member
3. First aid kit location: [posted on board]
4. AED location: [posted on board]
5. Complete incident report

### Power Outage
1. Turn off all power tools
2. Evacuate if no emergency lighting
3. Do not restart work until full power restored
4. Check that tools reset properly before resuming

### Tool Accidents
1. Stop work immediately
2. Secure the area
3. Report to shop manager
4. Do not move injured person unless necessary
5. Preserve the scene if serious injury
6. Complete incident report

## Amendments

These rules may be updated by the board with 30 days notice to members. Members will be notified of changes via email and posted notices.

Current members are responsible for staying informed of rule changes.

## Acknowledgment

All members must acknowledge they have read and understood these rules:
- Upon joining (new members)
- Annually (all members)
- After significant rule changes

---

**Questions about these rules?** Contact the shop manager or board members.

**Safety concerns?** Report immediately - anonymity respected.
